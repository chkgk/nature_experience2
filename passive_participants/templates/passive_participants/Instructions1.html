The active participant is a player in a game. Here are the rules.

There is the player, a co-player, and a virtual urn that contains 100 balls, some red, some green.

For each player, three factors together determine the payoff:

  1. The draw of a ball from the urn,
  2. the player's own action, and
  3. their co-player’s action.

The player and the co-player take one of two actions, either A or B. The urn from which the ball is drawn contains 20 red balls and 80 green balls. The color of the draw and the other player's action are revealed at the end of the round.

If a red ball is drawn, the actions of the player and the co-player do not matter for the outcome. The player receives nothing (US$ 0). The passive participant also "receives" nothing (US$ 0).

If a green ball is drawn, then the actions of the player and the co-player matter for the outcome. There are four possibilities:

  • A green ball is drawn and the player’s action is A and the co-player’s action is A:
    The player receives US$ 1. The passive participant also receives US$ 1.
  • A green ball is drawn and the player’s action is A and the co-player’s action is B:
    The player receives US$ 1. The passive participant receives US$ 3.
  • A green ball is drawn and the player’s action is B and the co-player’s action is A:
    The player receives US$ 3. The passive participant receives US$ 1.
  • A green ball is drawn and the player’s action is B and the co-player’s action is B:
    The player receives nothing (US$ 0). The passive participant also receives nothing (US$ 0).

The co-players’ action is programmed to be A in about 60 out of 100 cases and B in about 40 out of 100 cases.

You are the passive participant.
The two rounds of the game have already been played and you have been matched with a random active participant. You will receive the payment from this game after filling out a short survey.
